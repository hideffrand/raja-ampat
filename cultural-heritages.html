    Discover Raja Ampat:

    Cultural Heritages

    Arborek Village is a designated tourist village in Raja Ampat Regency, West Papua Province, renowned for its cultural and natural attractions. With an area of 7.2 hectares and a population of 44 households, the village is primarily inhabited by fishermen and entrepreneurs engaged in tourism.


    • Arborek Island
    • Culture Learning
    • Handcrafts


    by Ministry of Tourism and Creative Economy Indonesia

    Arborek Island

    Accessible from Sorong via a 3-4 hour ferry ride to Waisai, followed by a 2-hour speedboat journey, the village boasts nine homestays and a dive center owned by local residents. Arborek is famous for its handcrafted Manta Ray hats made from pandan leaves, as well as its traditional dances performed by local children.



    How to get there:


    Boat from Sorong Port to Arborek Island


    The Handcraft Masters!

    The Arborek people, are famous for their skill in crafting handcrafted hats made from pandan leaves. These hats are intricately woven to resemble the majestic shape of manta rays, reflecting the close connection between the villagers and the marine life surrounding their island home.


    The art of creating Manta Ray hats has been passed down through generations, showcasing the cultural heritage and artistic prowess of the Arborek community.
